Hi there, I´m Amelie!

I’m a UI/UX designer working in the automotive industry at Mercedes-Benz, where I mainly design mobile app experiences. I’m part of a DevOps team and work closely with other designers, developers, product managers, and various stakeholders. I really enjoy this kind of collaborative environment, because the best ideas usually come from working through things together, not in isolation. And yes, the whole “designers vs. developers” dynamic isn’t just an internet meme, anyone who’s worked in this kind of setup knows how real (and sometimes challenging it can be). But that’s exactly what I enjoy about it. Bringing together different perspectives, constraints, and expertise often leads to better, more thoughtful solutions and helps to get the most out of a product.

Over time, I’ve also gained experience in other areas like head unit design, as well as web design, which helps me think across different platforms and contexts. In addition to design, I have a basic understanding of software, app, and web development. I’m not a developer, but knowing how things are built helps me create more realistic, feasible solutions and communicate better with the team.

Over time, I’ve gained experience across different areas, including head unit design and web design, which helps me think beyond a single platform and consider how experiences connect across contexts. A big part of how I approach design is keeping the bigger picture in mind: for me, the overall brand experience is just as important as the individual product. That often means thinking beyond a single feature or screen and making sure there is a consistent, cohesive experience across different touchpoints. User research is a valuable part of my process. I’ve planned and conducted usability tests and use the insights to support and validate design decisions where it adds value. For me, it’s an important tool to better understand users and continuously refine solutions.

In addition to my design work, I have a basic understanding of software, app, and web development. There are definitely far better developers out there than me, but having that technical foundation allows me to think in more realistic, feasible solutions and collaborate more effectively with engineering teams. I don’t just have a Bachelor’s degree in Mobile Media and professional experience as a UI/UX designer, but also a background in business, including a commercial apprenticeship and practical experience in that field. This helps me understand not only user needs, but also business goals and constraints — and find solutions that balance both.

Outside of work, I spend time with my two cats or recharge by being near water — rivers, lakes, or the sea. It’s the easiest way for me to slow down and clear my head, and often where my best ideas and fresh perspectives come naturally.

I’m always happy to connect.

My values

harmony

For me, harmony is much more than a feeling, it is often the small things that make harmony complete. Harmony describes a balance of different parts that fit together, complement each other and together form a balanced unit.

curiosity

Children are curious and want to understand how something works. I have never lost this aspect in the course of my life. The excitement of trying new things and the desire to learn new insights drives me constantly. For me, it's like an "engine" for the development of my projects.

honesty

From my point of view, honesty stands for sincerity, openness, straightforwardness and fairness. Not only in private, but also in the professional environment, this is essential. Honesty requires a high degree of self-reflection and sovereignty, as it also includes admitting one's own mistakes and dealing with them openly.

quality

Quality means for me that work will be completed with with the highest requirement to the fullest satisfaction. This includes not only the completion of actual work but also reliability and transparency.
