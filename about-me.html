Hello, I´m Amelie!

I’m a UI/UX designer working in the automotive industry at Mercedes-Benz, where I mainly design mobile app experiences. I’m part of a DevOps team and work closely with other designers, developers, product managers, and various stakeholders. I really enjoy this kind of collaborative environment, because the best ideas usually come from working through things together, not in isolation. And yes, the whole “designers vs. developers” dynamic isn’t just an internet meme, anyone who’s worked in this kind of setup knows how real (and sometimes challenging) it can be. But that’s exactly what I enjoy about it. Bringing together different perspectives, constraints, and expertise often leads to better, more thoughtful solutions and helps to get the most out of a product.

How I work

I’ve gained experience across different areas, including head unit design and web design, which helps me think beyond a single platform and consider how experiences connect across contexts. A big part of how I approach design is keeping the bigger picture in mind: for me, the overall brand experience is just as important as the individual product. That often means thinking beyond a single feature or screen and making sure there is a consistent, cohesive experience across different touchpoints.
User research is also a valuable part of my process. I’ve planned and conducted usability tests and use the insights to support and validate design decisions where it adds value. For me, it’s an important tool to better understand users and continuously refine solutions.

Design, tech & business

In addition to my UI/UX related skills, I have a basic understanding of software, app, and web development. I am sure: there are definitely far better developers out there than me, but having that technical foundation allows me to think in more realistic, feasible solutions and collaborate more effectively with engineering teams.
I don’t just have a Bachelor’s degree in Mobile Media and professional experience as a UI/UX designer, but also a background in business, including a commercial apprenticeship and practical experience in that field. This helps me understand not only user needs, but also business goals and constraints — and find solutions that balance both.

And outside of work?

Outside of work, I spend time with my two cats or recharge by being near water — rivers, lakes, or the sea. It’s the easiest way for me to slow down and clear my head, and often where my best ideas and fresh perspectives come naturally.

I’m always happy to connect.

My values

Connected experience thinking

I think beyond individual screens and consider how everything connects. Creating a consistent experience across touchpoints is just as important as the details.

Pragmatic problem solving

I focus on solutions that actually work in real-world contexts, not just in theory. For me, good design is clear, usable, and feasible to build.

Balancing user, business, and technology

I aim to find solutions that work for users while also aligning with business goals and technical constraints. Good design sits right in the middle of all three.

Collaborative mindset

I enjoy working closely with different disciplines and value open exchange. The best ideas usually come from combining perspectives, not working in isolation.
