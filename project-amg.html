BEAST@AMG was a student project collaboration with Hochschule der Medien and Mercedes-Benz AMG. We develop an innovative home charging concept for electric vehicles.

Team and Planning

The interdisciplinary project involved around 11 students from different courses of study of Media University Stuttgart, Germany. The team was divided into different departments (for example Concept Team, Digital Team, Physical Team).
As a member of the Concept Team, I contributed to the development of a concept that seamlessly integrates your AMG into your lifestyle, creating a unique bond between you and your vehicle. The 'Spirit' encapsulates your AMG's essence, transforming your garage to match your passions and preferences. This approach not only provides efficient charging but also enriches the overall ownership experience.

The Concept

In an era where electric vehicles are becoming increasingly popular, home charging is playing an ever-greater role.

The concept for the AMG portfolio revolves around an innovative and immersive charging experience, personalized interactions, and a dynamic spirit interface. The core elements for the Concept is the Personalisation, an Entity named Spirit and a charging hub.

Concept-Team Approach

Within the Concept Team, we implemented several key components to develop our innovative home charging concept. We created a detailed Customer Journey Map to visualize the user's experience from start to finish. We developed two Personas to represent our target users and guide our design decisions. To ensure clear communication, we compiled a Wording Catalogue, providing definitions for important terms to help our customers better understand the terminology. Additionally, we established a comprehensive list of Interaction Rules to define and understand the behavior of the AMG Spirit, ensuring a seamless and intuitive user experience. This thorough approach allowed us to design a concept that truly integrates the AMG into the lifestyle of its users.

Project Outcome

The Digital Team successfully implemented our concept by translating it into a VR environment, allowing users to experience it firsthand through a VR headset. This immersive VR space provided a tangible preview of the innovative home charging solution we envisioned. Additionally, the Physical Team conducted user surveys to capture expectations and impressions, ensuring that our concept met user needs and preferences. This comprehensive approach ensured that our concept was not only innovative but also user-centered and practical.
